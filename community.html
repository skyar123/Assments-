# ask-the-team   -   Post a question for Skylar, Gabriel, Omi, or James
KL
KatieLynn_ECE Today at 9:14 AM
Has anyone had success using BEARS with a child who's resistant to stuffed animals entirely? My 4-year-old student just isn't connecting with the bear.
SB
Skylar Belt Team Today at 9:31 AM
Great question! We've had this come up a lot. A few options: try a small figurine or a pocket-sized object the child already loves. The attachment object doesn't have to be a bear - the bear is the vehicle for the reflection, not the relationship. What does this child gravitate toward in free play?
MR
Maria_R Today at 9:44 AM
We used a small dinosaur figure last year with a kiddo who loved dinos - worked beautifully. The teacher named it and it became part of their classroom community just like a bear would.
GG
Gabriel Guyton Team Today at 10:02 AM
Exactly this. BEARS is a framework, not a script. The object is a projection screen - it just needs to be something the child can safely put their feelings onto. Trust what you know about this child.
