Our Foundational Philosophy

What is a Circle of Communication?

Our name isn’t metaphorical—it’s clinical. Rooted in the P.L.A.Y. Project and DIR/Floortime model, a “circle of communication” is the fundamental unit of human social connection. A circle opens when one person makes an overture—a word, a gesture, a glance—and closes when the other person acknowledges and responds. Our work is about multiplying these moments.

Following the Child’s Lead

We observe what a child is already doing in their comfort zone to find natural openings for engagement—rather than imposing our agenda on the interaction.

Playful Obstruction

By gently and playfully impeding a child’s goal, we compel them to open a new circle of communication to negotiate the obstacle—turning a routine moment into a rich exchange.

Taffy Pulling

We strategically elongate interactions, transforming a single exchange into a multi-step sequence of mutual engagement—building the child’s capacity for increasingly complex communication.

Expectant Waiting

After making an overture, we pause—deliberately transferring the interactive initiative to the child and communicating trust in their ability to respond.

ConnectEd Circles trains educators and caregivers to exponentially increase the frequency and complexity of these interactions, facilitating higher functional developmental levels in young children.

Featured Program

The BEAR Program

Developed at Bank Street College’s Center for Emotionally Responsive Practice, the BEAR Program places a personal teddy bear in every child’s hands—creating a portable emotional anchor that helps children express feelings, navigate transitions, and build relationships.

ConnectEd Circles delivers BEAR workshops and coaching follow-ups in partnership with Buncombe Partnership for Children and Raising Resilience WNC, training educators across the region to integrate this evidence-based approach into their classrooms.
