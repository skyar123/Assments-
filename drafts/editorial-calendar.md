# Field Notes Editorial Calendar

A running queue for Substack + the ConnectEd Circles blog. Not a commitment
— adjust, cut, reorder freely. Tags: **Parent / Educator / Both** by audience
and the theorist(s) each post leans on.

The logic: Pillar posts run monthly (~1,500–2,500 words, evergreen, category
anchors). Field Notes run biweekly (~500–900 words, lyrical, Skylar-voice
first-person). Alternate them on a two-week drumbeat; slot seasonal items to
the month they belong to.

**The three posts to write in the first six months** (for service pipeline
and search visibility): #1 Being With, #2 BEARS framework, #5 Autism: It
Makes Sense! — oh wait, we don't have that one on request. Substitute #6
(Classrooms That Hold after Helene) instead. Those three publish first.

## Pillar posts — monthly, longer, category-anchoring

1. **What "Being With" actually looks like on a Tuesday afternoon**
   Both. Koplow, ERP.
   The definitional post. Opens the Being With category. Anchors everything
   that references the phrase after this. Second piece to publish, right
   after the Living Room article.

2. **The BEARS framework: what the bear is actually doing in the room**
   Educator. Koplow's *Bears, Bears Everywhere!*, ERP, Winnicott.
   Program explainer. Doubles as the marketing asset for BEAR workshop
   inquiries. Links directly to the bears.html page and the glossary entries
   for transitional objects and Being With.

3. **Why we don't use the word "behavior" in our house**
   Both. Delahooke, Porges, Dana.
   Stance piece. Rejects compliance-based models and connects
   behavior-as-communication to polyvagal theory in plain language. No
   jargon. No shaming the parent who still uses the word.

4. **Identity-first language and the double empathy problem**
   Both. Damian Milton, Chapman, neurodiversity scholarship.
   Why "autistic child" not "child with autism." Why the burden of
   understanding goes both directions. Written for educators who genuinely
   want to get this right and are nervous about getting it wrong.

5. ~~Autism: It Makes Sense!~~ *Per Skylar, no workshop landing page yet.*
   **Replacement: Stimming as communication — a guide for educators who
   were never taught this.**
   Educator. ASAN, Kapp, Delahooke.
   Retrains educators to read stimming as regulation and expression, not
   interruption. Includes what *not* to do.

6. **Classrooms that hold: what Asheville teachers needed after Helene**
   Educator. Koplow's *Creating Schools That Heal*, Perry, Lenore Terr.
   One-year reflection on the BEAR Program's Asheville rollout. Honest,
   local, grief-literate. Ties ConnectEd Circles publicly to the Helene
   response work.

7. **The Me Check: co-regulating with your own nervous system first**
   Educator. Porges, Dana, Delahooke.
   Concrete practice for teachers. Pairs with a free one-pager download
   (laminate-able card for the clipboard). Consider for the lead-magnet
   sequence.

8. **When grandparents visit and everything falls apart**
   Parent. Circle of Security, Siegel & Bryson.
   Why "regressions" around family visits are developmentally coherent, not
   failures. The reframe: the child is actually reorganizing safely because
   you're there.

9. **Drop-off rituals, and what they actually do**
   Both. Winnicott (transitional objects), Circle of Security.
   Practical morning-arrival script for educators + why the same four-second
   goodbye matters from the parent side. (Draft exists: see
   drafts/field-notes-drop-off-rituals.html.)

10. **Sleep regressions are not regressions**
    Parent. Circle of Security, Shonkoff.
    The reframe: nervous-system reorganization looks like disruption because
    it is. Freeing for exhausted parents who think they broke something.

## Lead-magnet one-pagers (from the site audit)

Four downloads that double as conference handouts. Each one gates a
newsletter signup on Substack.

- **BEARS in the Classroom Intro PDF** — 8 pages, one per core practice
  plus an overview and a Me Check bookmark.
- **Me Check one-pager** — single laminate-able card, cues co-regulation
  before the teacher addresses the child's feeling.
- **Drop-Off Ritual Card** — parent-facing, printable, wallet-sized.
- **Neurodiversity-Affirming Language Glossary** — two-page plain-language
  primer (identity-first vs. person-first, stimming, co-regulation, presume
  competence, double empathy).

## Notes on cadence

- **First six months** — priority order: Living Room (done) → #1 Being With
  → #11 Mason Jar Lids part two → #2 BEARS framework → #9 Drop-off rituals
  → #6 Classrooms That Hold (Helene). Roughly biweekly.
- **Seasonal anchors** — drop into the month: #12 August, #13 June,
  #14 June, #16 December, #17 September/October.
- **Evergreen** — everything else can float. Slot to the week you have
  bandwidth.
- **Related-post surfacing** — at the end of every post link to one pillar
  and one Field Note by category, plus one glossary entry by tag.
